02 — How I think

Most data is noise.
My job is the signal inside it.

I'm not interested in the clean, tidy version of a problem. Real data is dirty, human and contradictory — and that's exactly where the interesting answers hide. I start in the mess, find the pattern, and then build the system that keeps finding it after I walk away.

That instinct runs from a 9,000-row spreadsheet to a live agentic AI answering questions about itself. Same obsession, different scale.

Start from the mess

Begin with the raw, broken, real dataset — not the sanitized one. The truth is in the outliers.

04 — The question

Can a language model tell when a person is quietly falling apart?

EIEF — a benchmark for emotional intelligence in large language models. 120 career-distress scenarios, a five-dimension scoring framework, and LLM-as-judge evaluation backed by ML classifiers that flag risk with 81% accuracy. Authored end-to-end and submitted to the IDEA 2026 conference. It's the one cluster in this whole space that never fully resolves — and that's the point.
